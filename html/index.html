"If we begin with the assumption that the visual system analyzes a spatial frequency in terms of the simple sum of the harmonics in it, the first step is to study the responses of the system to simple sine-wave gratings."
"Contrast and Spatial Frequency" Scientific American, November 1974

In this essay, I hope to:

  1. Praise the sine wave for its ability to make a claim seem correct.
  2. Find the best terminology and images for thinking of those areas currently described by "spatial frequency channels", "contrast", "sine wave grating".
  3. Run circles around previous coverage of this topic, using interactive images to explore and demonstrate.
